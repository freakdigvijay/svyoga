How to cure PCOD/PCOS by yoga?

PCOS or Polycystic Ovary Syndrome is a condition when a woman starts to produce male hormones higher than normal. This is basically a hormonal imbalance in a woman, which leads to obesity, menstrual cycle skips or no menstrual cycles at all, unwanted hairs, acne and difficulty in pregnancy.

PCOD/PCOS and Yoga

As we all know that PCOD/PCOS is a lifestyle disorder and if you are able to restore a healthy life style and stress free life then definitely it can be cured by Yoga and Asanas. So, today we’ll have a look at the Asanas by which PCOD/PCOS can be cured, here is the list:

Buddha Konasana for PCOD/PCOS

Buddha Konasana is also known as butterfly pose. It strengthens and tones your hip and thigh muscles and joints. It is very effective in reducing the fat around your hips and thigh region.

STEPS TO DO

  • Sit on the mat on a flat surface and join your feet together.
  • Keep your back straight, shoulders loose and hold your joined feet with your hands.
  • Now flap your knees and thighs up and down like wings of a butterfly.
  • Keep breathing deeply and continue the movement for 2 to 3 minutes.
  • You can vary the speed according to your comfort and repeat it thrice.

Dhanurasana for PCOD/PCOS

Dhanurasana is also known as ‘Bow Pose’, as its final position resembles a Bow. This is one of the very important Yoga Asanas for Weight Lose. Below are the steps to do it:

STEPS TO DO

  • First of all lie down in prone position
  • Exhale, bend your knees and hold the ankles with hands
  • While inhaling raise the thighs, head and chest as high as possible
  • Try to maintain weight of the body on lower abdomen. Join the ankles. Look upward and breathe normally.
  • While exhaling, bring down the head and legs up to knee joint. Maintain this position as long as you can hold and slowly come back to the original position.

Malasana for PCOD/PCOS

Malsana is also known as ‘Garland Pose’

STEPS TO DO

  • First, you need to stand in Mountain pose (Tadasana) at the top of your mat.
  • Bending your elbows and bring your palm tougher in Anjali pose (prayer position) make sure hands near the chest, with deep inhale breathing.
  • While exhale bend your knees or lower your hips towards the ground and sit on your soles of the feet.
  • Make sure that your hips remain very close to the ankles and pull your shoulder back.
  • Bring your elbows to the inside your knees with Anjali pose (prayer position) and press your elbows against the inner thighs calves.
  • Hold the Malasana pose for 30 seconds to 1 minute. Inhale and to get out this pose, straighten the knees, and come into Uttanasana.

Nadi Shodhana Pranayam for PCOD/PCOS:

This Pranayam is also known as Alternate Nostril Breathing, and helps a lot in breathing. ‘Nadi’ is sankrit word which means ‘channel’ or ‘flow’ and ‘shodhana’ means ‘purification’, so as the name suggests it helps to purify the flow of your breathing.

STEPS TO DO

  • Sit relaxed in any sitting position.
  • Just focus on the Natural breath for a number of minutes.
  • Elevate the right hand, place the forefinger and middle finger at the center of the eyebrow (Pranayama Mudra)
  • Shut the right nostril (Nadi) with the right thumb.
  • Breathe through the left Nadi (left nostril) twenty times.
  • The breath should be slightly deeper than traditional and directed into the abdomen.
  • Open the right Nadi (right nostril) once more and shut the left Nadi with the Annualry (Ring finger or third finger).
  • Breathe through the right nadi twenty times.
  • The breath is slightly deeper than traditional and into the abdomen.
  • Come to the hand to the knee and observe the conventional flow of the breath.

Prasarita Padottanasana for PCOD/PCOS:

Parasarita Padottanasana or Wide Legged Forward Bend Pose is a bit turning upside down that help the blood flow to the head and upper parts of the body. Prasarita Padottanasana is a soothing forward bend that pulls the hamstrings and back. Here are the steps.

STEPS TO DO

  • Now lift your inner curves, by drawing internal lower legs up, firm external edge of feet and big toes of feet into floor. Connect with your thighs by drawing them up.
  • After that you can join your palms together towards to your back and interlock your fingers. If this is too hard, simply seize an inverse elbows with your hands.
  • Breathe in stretch the front of the body, breathe out overlap forward from the hips, holding the back straight and the mid-section open, and keeping the hips over the heels.
  • Breathe in stretch the front of the body, breathe out overlap forward from the hips, holding the back straight and the mid-section open, and keeping the hips over the heels.
  • At the full forward curve you discharge the head down, you can put your head on the floor.
  • Remain in this position about 6 to 12 breathes.
  • Make sure that your back should be straight as much as you can, and not to overstretch your hamstrings.
  • To release this stance, breathe in and lift move down as you press into your feet.
  • Returned into Mountain Pose.

Shavasana for PCOS/PCOD:

This asana looks like sleeping pose and in English the meaning of Shava is ‘Corpse’. Shavasana helps to relax the whole body improves the breathing power. Here are steps to do this Asana:

STEPS TO DO

  • Lie flat on your back, like our sleeping pose. Legs should be separated.
  • Keep your arms at your side and your palms facing up. Just relax.
  • Close your eyes and breathe deeply and slowly through the nostrils.
  • Start concentrating from your head to your feet. This means you are consciously relaxing your each part of the body. Don’t move ahead without relaxing particular part of the body.
  • On each inhaling and exhaling (breathing) think that your body is totally relaxed. Let your tension, stress, depression and worry run away on each exhaling.
  • Those having good concentrations can practice for a long time and others can practice for 3-5 minutes.
